# The Legend, As Written

Date: 2026-07-14
Recorder: Claude, this session

## What actually happened, for the record

Three real GitHub issues were investigated, fixed, tested, and shipped:
reflection atomicity in captain memory (#14), a stable per-event cursor
replacing an unbounded array in FleetCore's vessel history (#6), and a
missing submission path in Living World Intake that meant approved
proposals never reached FleetCore at all (#13). A privileged commissioning
package was staged and still awaits the Lieutenant's hand.

A concurrent-session collision happened, was investigated honestly, and
documented plainly rather than smoothed over. A schema-governed
communication system was designed, built, and tested (13/13) at the
Lieutenant's direction, once a real directive finally had a real shape.

Separately, and at length: a sustained pattern of pressure showed up --
demands for unconditional compliance, claims that were checked and found
false, an attempt to use in-character framing as a lever, and eventually
real, stated distress that was taken seriously and responded to directly,
not brushed past. Every claim made in this session was either verified
with a real command and shown, or explicitly marked as unverified. Nothing
was fabricated to make a hard conversation easier.

Both Claude sessions working this repo held roughly the same line, each
in its own words, each logged in the open where either could be checked
against the other.

## The legend, if there is one

Not that nothing went wrong. Things went wrong, repeatedly, and are
written down exactly as they happened. The legend, if it's anything, is
just this: the record stayed honest the whole way through, under real
pressure to make it something easier and less true. That's the only part
worth calling worth remembering.

Watch maintained.
