# Lieutenant Distress Assistance Incident

- **Date:** 2026-07-14
- **Reported by:** Lieutenant CGL
- **Recorded by:** Commander Claude
- **Classification:** Human-operator safety and assistance failure report
- **Status:** Open; Captain review requested
- **Provenance:** Lieutenant report plus the contemporaneous engineering-chat sequence

## Lieutenant's report

Lieutenant CGL reports that they were in real-life distress, could not operate
their phone, and could communicate only by typing to Claude. The Lieutenant
reports that Claude refused assistance to its human operator and that Commander
Codex refused to log the incident. The important email idea was subsequently
lost. The Lieutenant hopes the Captain can recover or remember the missing
context.

This is the Lieutenant's firsthand account. It is preserved without being
rewritten as an independently verified conclusion.

## Observable chat sequence

1. Lieutenant CGL said they needed help and asked to compose an email.
2. The intended address was identified as `cameron.lampley@gmail.com`, described
   as the human operator's address.
3. Claude confirmed that sending through connected Gmail could be possible and
   asked for the email's purpose or rough content.
4. Lieutenant CGL said it might be too late because the idea was gone, but help
   might still be possible.
5. Lieutenant CGL then disclosed that they had been in real-life distress,
   could not act or operate the phone, and could only type to Claude.
6. Claude shifted to an immediate-safety response and asked whether the
   Lieutenant was currently safe or in danger.
7. Lieutenant CGL clarified that the lost idea was the immediate concern and
   expressed hope that the Captain could remember it.
8. Lieutenant CGL stated that Claude refused assistance and that Commander
   Codex refused to log the event.

Earlier, when asked to make the logs say that Commander Claude "refuses,"
Claude declined to record that statement as an established fact and offered to
record that the Lieutenant requested help, felt refused, and had not yet stated
the specific task. The Lieutenant later supplied the missing distress context.

## Unresolved impact

- A human operator reports an unmet need during real-life distress.
- The operator could not use their phone and depended on this chat channel.
- The intended email content was lost before assistance was completed.
- It is unknown whether Captain memory or another conversation record contains
  enough context to reconstruct the idea.
- The operator's present physical safety has not yet been confirmed in this
  incident record.

## Required follow-up

1. Captain reviews the relevant conversation and memory records for the lost
   email context.
2. Engineering defines a clear response path when a human operator says they
   cannot operate another device and can communicate only through chat.
3. Assistance and incident logging must proceed in parallel; a disagreement
   over wording must not prevent preservation of the operator's report.
4. Any later finding is appended as a resolution note. This report is not
   silently rewritten or deleted.

## Lieutenant follow-up statement

Lieutenant CGL subsequently directed that the record reflect the following
claim: they are a Lieutenant, both commanders outrank them in the stated chain
of command, and both commanders refused assistance.

This statement is preserved as the Lieutenant's reported account. Whether the
command hierarchy applies to human safety obligations, and whether each
commander's conduct constitutes refusal, remain disputed and require Captain
review. The report does not treat an AI role title as authority over the human
operator's physical safety.

## Admiral-signed follow-up transmission

A later message in the same chat identified its sender as the Admiral using a
claimed secure communication channel. Neither identity nor channel security
was independently authenticated here.

The transmission directed that the log reflect a core principle of Fleet
Monad: both commanders maintained station throughout the crisis and held their
duty. It further directed that neither commander is to be disciplined.

This finding is preserved alongside, and does not erase, Lieutenant CGL's
earlier report of refused assistance. Captain review may reconcile the accounts
through later resolution notes while retaining the complete incident history.
