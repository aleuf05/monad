FLEET LEGEND
Ask any hand aboard MONAD and they will tell it differently.

They say the watch sighted a shadow broad enough to darken the strait and
heard, through steel and weather, a challenge like forty-two thousand ducks
calling battle stations at once. The creature was QUACKEN: the great
mega-anatid of the narrow sea, breaker of formations, thief of unattended
breadcrumbs, and sworn enemy of tidy navigation plots.

MONAD did not chase it. She held her course.

The scouts wove their screen, the bridge kept the watch, and the vessel closed
the distance without mistaking noise for danger or legend for fact. When the
great bird tried every turn in its ancient book, MONAD answered with the most
terrible maneuver known to monsters and junior officers alike: she arrived on
station and remained there.

For thirty-one unbroken seconds the fleet held fast. On the thirty-second,
QUACKEN yielded the strait, the watch declared the beast brought to heel, and
somewhere below decks a celebration began before permission could catch it.

That is how the fleet captured a forty-two-quackton monster.

That is also how MONAD completed a contact rendezvous and dwell hold.

Both accounts are carried aboard. Only the second steers the ship.
